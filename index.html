Featured Speakers

Yochai Benkler

Berkman Professor of Entrepreneurial Legal Studies at Harvard Law School

Benkler studies commons-based peer production, and published his seminal book "The Wealth of Networks" in 2006.

Kilnam Chon

Internet Pioneer

Kilnam Chon helped bring the Internet to Asia and is an outspoken advocate for the open web and digital commons. In 2012, he was inducted into the inaugural class of the Internet Society's (ISOC) Internet Hall of Fame.

SohYeong Noh

Director at Art Center Nabi and a board member of CC

As the main venue for new media art production in Korea, Nabi promotes cross-disciplinary collaboration and understanding among science, technology, humanities, and the arts.

Julia Leda

President of Young Pirates of Europe

European organisation, political democracy and participation of youth through online and other major concern. Leda's report outlining potential changes to EU copyright law was accepted by the Parliament in July.

Lila Tretikov

Executive Director of the Wikimedia Foundation

Lila Tretikov is the Executive Director of the Wikimedia Foundation, the nonprofit organization that operates Wikipedia. Wikipedia is freely available in 290 languages and used by nearly half a billion people around the world every month.

Ryan Merkley

CEO of Creative Commons, ex-COO of the Mozilla Foundation

Ryan has been leading open source projects at the Mozilla Foundation such as the open source movement.
